Speaking to my brother in law this morning, we discussed writing and publishing online. I mentioned [POSSE](https://indieweb.org/POSSE) - **P**ublish (on your) **S**ite, **S**yndicate **E**verywhere. So below are a few quick thoughts on the matter.

## Why?
Owning your content is the first and best reason. If it's on your own website, then it becomes the primary, searchable repositary for your writing. If anyone wants to find out more - they go to your website. If a platform shuts down, changes their algorithm, no longer aligns with your beliefs - you can stop writing there, but all your content is still in a location of your choosing: your website.

## How?
The POSSE website has a nice [how to](https://indieweb.org/POSSE#How_to). But it doesn't cover how to syndicate everywhere from a platform like [Ghost](https://ghost.org/), for example.

Now, Ghost has a [plugin for Buffer](https://ghost.org/integrations/buffer/) which can handle that part for you. Having a quick look at the documenation makes it look like it's not the easiest or most straight forward if you don't want to spend time wiring together many services...

But honestly, there's an opportunity here when it comes to generative AI and LLMs. I understand that people feel the need to play the social media game - especially where business is involved. So the following flow might actually fulfill quite a few needs:

1. Write your content, how you want it, on your own site.
2. Create a series of prompts, one (or more) for each socila media you want to post to
3. Put your content into your LLM of choice, with the prompt, and get it to spit out the algorithm optimised version of your post.
4. Go and manually post it on that network.

This unlocks something nice: your writing/thoughts are no longer influenced by the incentives of the platform on which your posting - let the genAI handle that. It's even a little poetic - let the genAI algorithm optimise posts so that the social media algorithm picks it up.
